# 📚 E-Learning Sales Analysis

A data-driven project analyzing learner spending, course performance, and category trends to uncover actionable insights for improving sales and engagement in an e-learning platform.

---

## 📌 Objectives
- Identify top-spending learners and regional spending patterns.
- Highlight best-selling courses and categories driving revenue.
- Detect underperforming courses and potential growth areas.
- Recommend marketing, cross-selling, and regional strategies.
- Monitor monthly trends for seasonal demand spikes.

---

## 🛠 Methodology
- Collected learner purchase and course sales data.
- Segmented analysis by **spending, course popularity, category performance, and country-level diversity**.
- Highlighted unsold courses to identify gaps in marketing or content alignment.
- Synthesized findings into actionable recommendations.

---

## 📊 Key Insights
- **Learner Spending**: Alice Johnson (USA) and Ravi Kumar (India) are top spenders.  
- **Top Courses**: *Python for Beginners* and *Advanced Excel Analytics* dominate sales.  
- **Category Performance**: Programming generates the highest revenue; Data Analysis also strong.  
- **Learner Diversity**: Many learners purchase across multiple categories → cross-selling opportunities.  
- **Unpurchased Courses**: Some courses remain unsold, signaling misalignment or weak promotion.

---

## ✅ Recommendations
- **Focus Marketing on Top Categories**: Promote Programming & Data Analysis courses.  
- **Bundle Offers**: Combine Python + Machine Learning for advanced learners.  
- **Cross-Selling Strategy**: Target learners with personalized offers in related categories.  
- **Country-Specific Campaigns**: Tailor promotions for India & USA to maximize conversions.  
- **Revitalize Underperforming Courses**: Adjust pricing, content, or offer trial modules.  
- **Monthly Trend Monitoring**: Track seasonal spikes to optimize marketing spend.

---

## 📈 Conclusion
Technical skill courses (Programming, Data Analysis) are the primary revenue drivers.  
Business and Marketing courses need stronger positioning.  
Leveraging **cross-selling** and **regional targeting** will maximize learner engagement and profitability.
